italic not italic↗

Roman Gornitsky

An exploration of how italics function as more than just a stylistic counterpart to roman type. Gornitsky talks about the tension and interplay between these forms, challenging conventional ideas about their roles and uncovering the deeper communicative possibilities of italics in modern typography.

a note on the type↗

Mark Owens

A reflective piece on the power of typography to shape meaning and emotion. Owens examines how typefaces operate as communicative tools, weaving together history, context, and culture to reveal the ways in which the form of letters influences the way we experience text.

typeface as programme↗

Jürg Lehni

Lehni reimagines typography as a process rather than a product, presenting typefaces as programmable systems that push the boundaries of traditional design. By blending art and technology, this article highlights the potential for type to become a medium of experimentation and dialogue, far beyond its surface-level aesthetics.

The three readings converge on the shared understanding that typography is inherently dynamic, evolving with its context and deeply embedded in its environment. They frame typography as more than a design tool—it's a medium for experimentation and commentary, shaping how readers engage with text. Together, the articles position typography as a dialogue, where designers, readers, and type itself collaboratively create meaning, reflecting its active role within broader systems.
